Help & Support

Getting Started

  1. Prepare Your Image
    Ensure you have a high-quality microscopic blood smear image. The image should be clear, well-focused, and show individual blood cells.
  2. Upload the Image
    Click the upload area or drag and drop your image file. Supported formats include PNG, JPG, and JPEG files up to 10MB.
  3. Analyze
    Click the "Analyze Image" button to start the AI analysis. Results will appear within seconds.
  4. Review Results
    Review the diagnosis results, confidence scores, and probability distributions. Always consult with medical professionals for clinical decisions.

Image Requirements

Microscopic blood smear images (thin or thick smears)
Clear, well-focused images with good contrast
Supported formats: PNG, JPG, JPEG
File size: Maximum 10MB
Resolution: Minimum 128x128 pixels (higher recommended)

Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate is the AI diagnosis?
Our AI model achieves 95.8% accuracy on test data, with 94.2% sensitivity and 97.1% specificity. However, this tool is designed for screening support and should not replace professional medical diagnosis.
What types of malaria can be detected?
The current model performs binary classification (Parasitized vs Uninfected) and can detect the presence of malaria parasites in blood cells. It does not differentiate between specific Plasmodium species.
Is my data stored or shared?
No, we prioritize your privacy. Uploaded images are processed securely and are automatically deleted after analysis. No patient data is stored on our servers.
Can I use this for clinical diagnosis?
This tool is designed for research and screening support only. It should not be used as the sole basis for clinical diagnosis. Always confirm results with qualified healthcare professionals and standard laboratory protocols.
What should I do if I get an error?
Common issues include unsupported file formats, files too large, or network connectivity problems. Ensure your image meets the requirements and try again. If problems persist, contact support.
How long does analysis take?
Analysis typically takes less than 3 seconds per image. Processing time may vary based on image size and server load.

Important Considerations

Medical Disclaimer
This AI tool is intended for research and screening support purposes only. It does not replace professional medical diagnosis, clinical judgment, or standard laboratory procedures. Always consult with qualified healthcare professionals and follow established clinical protocols for malaria diagnosis and treatment decisions.
Best Practices
For optimal results, use high-quality microscopic images with proper staining (Giemsa stain recommended). Ensure images are well-focused and show clear cellular details. Consider multiple image analysis for comprehensive screening.
